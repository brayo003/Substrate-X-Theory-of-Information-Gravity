# OBSERVATIONAL REFERENCES

## Mercury Perihelion Precession
- **Observed Value**: 43.0 ± 0.1 arcseconds/century
- **Primary Reference**: Clemence, G. M. (1947). "The Relativity Effect in Planetary Motions". *Reviews of Modern Physics* 19, 361.
- **Modern Value**: Park, R. S., et al. (2017). "Precession of Mercury's Perihelion from Ranging to the MESSENGER Spacecraft". *Astronomical Journal* 153, 121.
- **Measurement Method**: Planetary ephemerides analysis
- **Uncertainty Source**: Solar system modeling and observational precision

## Gravitational Lensing
- **Observed Value**: 1.75 ± 0.05 arcseconds  
- **Primary Reference**: Dyson, F. W., Eddington, A. S., & Davidson, C. (1919). "A Determination of the Deflection of Light by the Sun's Gravitational Field". *Philosophical Transactions* A 220, 291-333.
- **Modern Confirmation**: Shapiro, S. S., et al. (2004). "Measurement of the Solar Gravitational Deflection of Radio Waves using Geodetic VLBI". *Physical Review Letters* 92, 121101.
- **Measurement Method**: Solar eclipse astrometry / VLBI
- **Uncertainty Source**: Astrometric precision and solar modeling

## Binary Pulsar Orbital Decay  
- **Observed Value**: -2.405 ± 0.005 × 10⁻¹² s/s
- **Primary Reference**: Weisberg, J. M., & Taylor, J. H. (2005). "The Relativistic Binary Pulsar B1913+16". *ASP Conference Series* 328, 25.
- **Latest Update**: Weisberg, J. M., Nice, D. J., & Taylor, J. H. (2010). "Timing Measurements of the Relativistic Binary Pulsar PSR B1913+16". *Astrophysical Journal* 722, 1030.
- **Measurement Method**: Pulsar timing observations
- **Uncertainty Source**: Timing precision and system modeling
