# Universal Multi-Domain Engine (UMDE)
## Three-Layer Architecture for Cross-Domain Dynamical Simulation

## Abstract
The Universal Multi-Domain Engine (UMDE) implements the SXC–IGC field equations through a strictly layered architecture that separates immutable universal dynamics from domain-specific adaptation and adaptive control. This separation guarantees mathematical invariance, numerical stability, and cross-domain applicability across physical, biological, financial, urban, and informational systems.

---

## 1. Architectural Overview

The UMDE is composed of three strictly separated layers. No layer may violate the constraints of the layer beneath it.

### Layer 1 — Core Physics (Immutable)

**Purpose**  
Execute the universal SXC–IGC partial differential equations.

**Inputs**
- Field arrays:  
  - ρ (density / system state)  
  - E (excitation / driving potential)  
  - F (inhibition / regulatory force)
- Scalar or field parameters:  
  - τ (time constants)  
  - M (stiffness / resistance)  
  - δ (driving coefficients)  
  - λ (damping coefficients)

**Outputs**
- Time-evolved field derivatives:  
  - ∂ρ/∂t  
  - ∂E/∂t  
  - ∂F/∂t

**Key Properties**
- Immutable equations
- No domain logic
- No neural control
- Numerical stability enforced via Implicit–Explicit (IMEX) time stepping
- Safe operation near critical regimes

---

### Layer 2 — Domain Adaptation (Configurable)

**Purpose**  
Map real-world data and constraints onto the universal fields without modifying the core equations.

**Inputs**
- Domain-specific datasets (e.g., financial indices, zoning maps, biological metrics, sensor networks)

**Outputs**
- Initial field conditions
- Spatially heterogeneous parameter fields:
  - τ(x)
  - M(x)
  - δ(x)

**Mechanisms**
1. **Heterogeneous Parameter Fields**  
   Parameters are defined as spatial arrays derived from empirical data.
2. **Non-linear Gating M(ρ)**  
   State-dependent stiffness producing threshold and tipping-point behavior.

**Constraint**
- Domain adaptation may only modify parameters, never equations.

---

### Layer 3 — Control & Interface (Adaptive)

**Purpose**  
Maintain operational stability and criticality through adaptive parameter modulation.

**Inputs**
- Monitoring metrics from Layer 1:
  - ρ_max
  - Stress
  - Activity

**Outputs**
- Parameter adjustment signals applied exclusively to Layer 2

**Key Components**
- **F2C Neural Controller**  
  External trained controller acting as a pilot, not a physicist.
- **Safety Envelope**  
  Global clamps, rollback mechanisms, and hard bounds preventing runaway instability.

**Constraint**
- No direct field manipulation permitted.

## 2. Mathematical Foundation: SXC–IGC Equations

### 2.1 Density Field Evolution

∂ρ/∂t = ∇·(D(ρ,E)∇ρ) + G(ρ,E) − L(ρ,F) − ρ/τ_ρ

**Terms**
- ∇·(D∇ρ): diffusion / flow
- G(ρ,E): growth or source term
- L(ρ,F): loss or regulation
- −ρ/τ_ρ: natural damping or leakage

---

### 2.2 Excitation Field Evolution

∂E/∂t = δ₁ρ − γ₁E + ∇²E

- Excitation generated by density
- Natural dissipation
- Spatial propagation

---

### 2.3 Inhibition Field Evolution

∂F/∂t = δ₂E − γ₂F + ∇²F

- Regulatory response to excitation
- Natural decay
- Spatial propagation
