# The Substrate X Axial-Vector Lagrangian

The total density $\mathcal{L}$ is given by:
$$\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_{SM} - \frac{1}{4}X_{\mu\nu}X^{\mu\nu} + \frac{1}{2}m_X^2 X_\mu X^\mu - X_\mu J_X^\mu$$

## 1. Field Strength
$X_{\mu\nu} = \partial_\mu X_\nu - \partial_\nu X_\mu$

## 2. The Interaction Current (Axial-Protophobic)
$J_X^\mu = \sum_{f} X_\mu \bar{\psi}_f \gamma^\mu \gamma^5 (g_f^V + g_f^A) \psi_f$

Where:
- $g_p \approx 0$ (Protophobic: Coupling to protons suppressed)
- $g_e \in [2\times 10^{-4}, 1.4\times 10^{-3}]$ (CERN NA64 Window)
- $g_n \approx 1.15 \times 10^{-3}$ (Atomki Resonance)

## 3. Mass Scaling
The $m_X$ term ($17.01$ MeV) is stabilized via the Stueckelberg scalar $\phi$, ensuring gauge invariance is maintained even at high energies.
